Sigilographic Digitalisation

Sigilographic Digitalisation is a project dedicated to the digitization and dissemination of sigillographic data — the study of seals and their impressions. This initiative creates a dynamic digital platform that allows both researchers and the public to explore these historical artifacts, their context, and their symbolic significance.

Reflection

This project holds the potential to greatly enhance the field of sigillography by providing a centralized, accessible archive for both scholarly research and public education. Digitizing these delicate artifacts ensures their preservation while opening doors to new interpretations, relational networks, and symbolic analysis.

Ontological Relation

This work bridges the historical with the digital, participating in the field of digital humanities. It serves as an ontological act — recontextualizing seals not just as objects, but as vessels of identity, memory, and authority across time.
